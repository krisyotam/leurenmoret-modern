![20160516 Snowden Archive ——The SidToday Files](../../assets/images/20160516-snowden-archive--_med.png)

**Learn More:**<https://theintercept.com/snowden-sidtoday/about/> | May, 16 **Contact:** <https://theintercept.com/snowden-sidtoday/about/#contact> | 2016

 _SIDtoday is the internal newsletter for the NSA’s most important division, the Signals Intelligence Directorate. After editorial review, The Intercept is releasing nine years’ worth of newsletters in batches, starting with 2003. The agency’s spies explain a surprising amount about what they were doing, how they were doing it, and why._

_The Intercept_ ’s first _SIDtoday_ release comprises 166 articles, including all articles published between March 31, 2003, when SIDtoday began, and June 30, 2003, plus installments of all article series begun during this period through the end of the year. Major topics include the National Security Agency’s role in interrogations, the Iraq War, the war on terror, new leadership in the Signals Intelligence Directorate, and new, popular uses of the internet and of mobile computing devices.  
  
Along with this batch, we are publishing the stories featured below, which explain how and why we’re releasing these documents, provide an overview of _SIDtoday_ as a publication, report on one especially newsworthy set of revelations, and round up other interesting tidbits from the files.
