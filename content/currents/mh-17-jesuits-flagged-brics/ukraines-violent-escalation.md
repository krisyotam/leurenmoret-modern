In less than a year, Ukraine has been plunged into a vortex of violence that doesn’t appear to have any end in sight. The vicious circle that started with Molotov cocktails thrown at riot police has now gone to ballistic missiles launched at cities.

[Follow RT's LIVE UPDATES on Kiev's bloody eastern Ukraine campaign](http://rt.com/news/eastern-ukraine-army-operation-680/)

The Ukrainian turmoil started in November 2013, after President Viktor Yanukovich put on a hold on a key [EU integration deal](http://rt.com/news/ukraine-eu-yanukovich-protest-368/). Thousands of Ukrainians, outraged by what they saw as betrayal of their aspirations, responded with mass protests in Kiev.

![2352491](/assets/images/pic-3-maidan-3_med_hr.jpeg)

  


Over the weeks the protests became [increasingly violent](http://rt.com/news/ukraine-violence-clashes-protest-562/), as right-wing radicals weighed in and the government attempted to tighten the screws. Eventually, Kiev turned into a scene of rioting, with protesters using Molotov Cocktails and guerrilla tactics to take over government offices.

  


![UKRAINE/](/assets/images/pic-4-maidan-5_med_hr.jpeg)

The violence spiraled [out of control](http://rt.com/news/rt-crew-kiev-maidan-898/) a day after [Yanukovich submitted](https://www.google.ru/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=&esrc=s&source=web&cd=4&cad=rja&uact=8&ved=0CD4QFjAD&url=http%3A%2F%2Frt.com%2Fnews%2Fukraine-president-opposition-sign-agreement-114%2F&ei=Pu7lU92wOMbIyAO9wIK4Aw&usg=AFQjCNEm5O3ygeiLKiMsbyGQK4Jp_zuRLw&sig2=KueglfVGu7hvthp9OqlxYg&bvm=bv.72676100,d.bGE) to virtually all the demands conveyed to him by parliamentary opposition leaders. Snipers started shooting at both the protesters and the police in Kiev, triggering a bloodbath and a collapse of the government. The identity of the snipers [remains unknown](http://rt.com/news/158864-kiev-snipers-not-berkut/).

  


![Pic 5. ts-1](/assets/images/pic-5-ts-1_med_hr.jpeg)

  


After the coup, the new authorities in Kiev took steps such as an attempt to strip the Russian language of its official status in largely Russian-speaking eastern regions. A protest movement [grew in response](http://rt.com/news/ukraine-donetsk-kharkov-nikolaev-161/), only to be dismissed and retaliated against with the dispersion of rallies, arrests and [kidnappings](http://rt.com/news/178560-amnesty-international-lyashko-abductions/) of protest leaders, and [attacks on local politicians](http://rt.com/news/ukraine-presidential-candidates-attacked-516/). Kiev pressure led to some protesters taking arms and storming local government offices much as their opponents had done months earlier.

  


![UKRAINE-CRISIS/](/assets/images/pic-6-maidan-8_med_hr.jpeg)

  


Kiev declared the militias terrorists and Russian mercenaries and launched a military crackdown in the defiant east. Armored infantry vehicles were sent to [cities like Mariupol](http://rt.com/news/157884-shooting-mariupol-eastern-ukraine/) in a demonstration of force, while mortar shells [started falling](http://rt.com/news/158132-slavyansk-fire-shooting-referendum/) on militia-erected roadblocks.

Instead of a quick victory, the so-called ‘anti-terrorist operation’ stalled, with militias capturing police armories, some military depots and even [the military hardware](http://rt.com/news/ukraine-troops-withdraw-slavyansk-940/) used against them. [Kiev’s response](http://rt.com/news/163352-ukraine-airstrike-lugansk-missiles/) was to deploy Su-24 bombers, Mig-29 fighters, Mi-24 helicopter gunships and other aircraft, since their military had unchallenged air superiority. Militias proved to have shoulder-launched SAMs in their arsenals.

  


![UKRAINE-CRISIS/](/assets/images/pic-9-maidan-9_med_hr.jpeg)

Angered by the armed resistance and hindered by the poor morale of their own troops, the Ukrainian government upped the violence ante. T-64BM Bulat [battle tanks](http://rt.com/news/154544-slavyansk-fighting-east-ukraine/) and heavy artillery pieces like 152 mm howitzers Msta and 152 mm field guns Giatsint-B joined APCs and mortars in the battlefield, laying waste to militia strongholds like Slavyansk.

Among the heavy weapons the Ukrainian army uses are BM-21 Grad and BM-27 Uragan [multiple-rocket launchers](http://rt.com/news/176676-humanitarian-catastrophe-eastern-ukraine/), a highly indiscriminate kind of weapon designed for destroying enemy forces in the field. If fired at a city, it cannot but cause multiple civilian casualties. Kiev denied doing so, but international rights group reported evidence to the contrary, adding that such actions may [amount to war crimes](http://rt.com/news/175484-hrw-rockets-killed-civilians/).
