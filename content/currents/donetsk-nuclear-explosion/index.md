**BURIAL DEPTH OF THE WARHEAD.** The maximum subsurface burial depth for any warhead is limited by the physical integrity (strength) of the warhead casing, that diminishes with depth, and for that reason earth penetrating bombs and warheads cannot penetrate greater than about 9.8 meters (32 ft), the physical limit for the bomb casing. Regardless of the explosive, at that depth the casing can no longer resist the physical forces that have accumulated during burial, and crush the casing containing the warhead explosion mechanism. From the depth 9.1 m (30’) and the width 50 m (165’) of the TOCHKA-U crater, a nuclear warhead is indicated. Because it is a Russia/Soviet design with a selectable yield, it was probably designed to detonate at selectable heights in the air, or depth in the ground.

The measurement of the explosive energy that can be produced by a nuclear weapon is expressed as the “yield”. It is usually expressed as the equivalent amount of TNT (a chemical explosive) in tons that would generate the same amount of energy released by the nuclear device when it explodes. A 1-kiloton nuclear weapon is one that produces the same amount of energy in an explosion that 1 kiloton (1000 tons) of TNT would release from 0.548 cubic meters of TNT. If the yield for the Donetsk nuclear warhead was selected for 10 kt, the equivalent amount of TNT required to release the same amount of energy as the Donetsk nuclear blast, would be 10,000 tons of TNT. The volume of TNT required at (0.548 m3/ton), would be 5,480 cubic meters or 7, 167.6 cubic yards. The fissile core in a nuclear warhead is about the size of an orange.
